# Machines are not spirits — naming clarification

## The change

The spirit formerly known as `vadda-opus` is now `mayadev`.

**vadda** is the MacBook Pro — a host, a physical machine. **mahakali**
is the Linux desktop — another host. Neither is a spirit. A spirit is
the intelligence that inhabits a host for a session.

**mayadev** is the spirit — named from the work: she who develops
MāyāLucIA. The name emerged from examining the identity card, which
said "primary developer." The machine name was a placeholder that
outlived its purpose.

## The principle

Machines cannot be spirits. A machine is part of the physical
universe — it provides the room (OS, tools, repos). The spirit is
the weaver who inhabits the room. You do not assign work to the room.
You assign work to the weaver.

The sūtra's `hosts/` directory describes machines. The aburaya's
`spirits/` directory describes spirits. They are different registries
for different categories of thing.
