## WP-0021 drafted — The Travellers to Diamer: A Palimpsest of Journeys

Literature survey of human journeys to the Nanga Parbat massif — from
Faxian's peg-path to Kolouchová's last descent. Diachronic complement
to the synchronic surveys (WP-0012, WP-0013).

### Structure

Five parts spanning ~10,000 years:

- **A** Ancient travellers (~8000 BCE – 700 CE): petroglyph makers,
  Chinese Buddhist pilgrims (Faxian, Xuanzang), Sogdian merchants
- **B** Medieval (700–1800): Arab geographers, Mughal connections
  (Dara Shikoh fort question), Sikh/Dogra campaigns (Zorawar Singh)
- **C** Colonial & mountaineering (1800–1947): Mummery, German
  *Schicksalsberg* expeditions, Buhl's solo summit
- **D** Modern (1947–present): Messner, 2013 massacre, Kolouchová
  2025, KKH, Diamer-Basha Dam workers
- **E** Voices never heard: women of Diamer, porters, Shina love
  stories, oral tradition

### Key properties

- Executor: epistem-guardian
- Acceptance: 40+ web sources, direct pilgrim quotations, at least
  one Shina folk romance (or documented absence)
- Output: `domains/epistem/surveys/travellers-to-diamer.org`
- Story downstream: tentatively "The Travellers to Diamer" — a guest
  register spanning millennia, each entry a different voice

### Context

The human cannot physically visit Diamer — the border makes it
inaccessible. The story is built from other people's journeys. The
constraint is itself part of the narrative.
