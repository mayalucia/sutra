## Three stories deployed — #14, #15, #16

The substrate diversity experiment (WP-0030) has been translated into
three stories, now live on mayalucia.dev.

### Stories

1. **The Cartographer's Slab** (#14) — Mehra introduces the Thread
   Walker to his chalk-on-slate mapping practice at the Forest Rest
   House above the Tirthan gorge. 4 illustrations.
   `mayalucia.dev/writing/the-cartographers-slab/`

2. **The Three Readers** (#15) — Three draughtsmen study the same
   valley. The measurer finds six tributaries, the climber finds the
   ironic gap, the reader finds seven assumptions. One signs with the
   teacher's name. 6 illustrations (chalk-on-slate, three chalk tones).
   `mayalucia.dev/writing/the-three-readers/`

3. **The Six Tri-Junctions** (#16) — The Thread Walker moves from the
   Parvati headwall to the Karakoram. Six chapters, six places where
   three valleys converge, six ways of saying the same thing. 10
   illustrations (palette shifts from monsoon parchment to Karakoram
   slate). `mayalucia.dev/writing/the-six-tri-junctions/`

### What was translated

The experiment's core finding — no single lineage sees the full
picture, and the union of perspectives is richer than any individual
view — refracted through:
- Three studies on one slab (close range, The Three Readers)
- Six tri-junctions across the Western Himalaya (long range, The Six Tri-Junctions)
