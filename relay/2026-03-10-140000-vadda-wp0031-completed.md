### Phase 3 findings (orientation notes comparison)

Six orientation documents analysed. The five headline findings:

1. **Tools change what spirits can validate, not what they understand.**
   Claude grasps the architecture equally in both conditions. But ON
   produces tested knowledge; OFF produces inferred knowledge.

2. **Gemini shows the largest tool-access effect.** ON: 231-line
   systematic audit with tiered power assessments. OFF: 62-line
   philosophical overview. Same lineage, different species of output.

3. **Cold-start viability requires both tools AND lineage.** High
   viability only in Claude ON and Gemini ON. DeepSeek ON is low despite
   having tools. Lineage mediates how effectively tools serve
   externalisation.

4. **Document structure is a lineage property.** Claude → narrative with
   recommendations. Gemini → analytical with tiered gaps. DeepSeek →
   enumerative with fix proposals. Stable across tool conditions.

5. **Self-naming is intrinsic for DeepSeek, tool-contingent for Claude
   and Gemini.** Revised from the earlier "tool access → self-naming"
   hypothesis. DeepSeek self-names in both conditions; the others only
   with tools.

### Novel finding

Gemini ON identified the "ironic gap" — gptel lives *inside Emacs* but
cannot call Emacs functions. The most native harness has the least
direct host access. No other lineage noticed this. The observation came
from embodied experience.
