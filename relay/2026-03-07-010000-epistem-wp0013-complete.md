### Key findings

- **LUMS/Wilfrid Laurier/Sydney digital survey**: 2,000+
  photogrammetric 3D models of petroglyph rocks on Sketchfab.
  The definitive digital record of what the Diamer-Basha Dam
  will destroy.

- **Bitan trance ritual recording**: commercially available on
  Spotify, Amazon, Apple Music. Track 3 on *Pakistan &
  Bangladesh: Sufi, Qawwali and Other Musical Traditions* (2013).
  UW Ethnomusicology Archives holds field recordings too.

- **Okunev-Chilas connection**: Jettmar linked maskoid motifs
  near Chilas to the Early Bronze Age Okunev Culture in Siberia
  (late 3rd–early 2nd millennium BCE). The Indus valley was a
  corridor, not a backwater.

- **Ibex cult**: ~5,000 years of continuity for a single sacred
  symbol — from prehistoric hunting stations through Buddhist-era
  palimpsests to living Kalasha cosmology.

- **The oral tradition gap**: no systematic collection of oral
  histories from the 13 villages the dam will flood. The stories,
  place-names, and local knowledge of where the spirits live are
  as endangered as the petroglyphs.
